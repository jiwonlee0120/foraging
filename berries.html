Forgaing

Berries contain seeds dense in nutrients and leaves and stem have strong fragrance.
Grows in moist foothill and montane woods.
Northern black currants may cause diarrhea and stomach upset if eaten in quantity.
Berries have thick skin and a mealy taste and dried berries can be popped when fried in grease over low flame.
Grows in dry open woods and gravelly or sandy soils in arctic and alpine regions.
Berries may cause nausea or constipation if eaten in quantity.
Berries have modest taste, tart if picked too early and contains high levels of pectin, which benefits making jams.
Two types of berries, one is from America and another is from EUropian country.
Eating gooseberries in quantity may cause stomach upset.
Ingredients
140 grams flour
500ml milk
3 Eggs
2 tbsp Sugar
150 grams of gooseberry Jam
90 grams Unsalted Butter

1, Blend the milk,eggs, and flour in the blender.

2, Pour the mixture through a fine sieve to remove any lumps and leave the batter on room temperature.

3,Heat the crepe pan, melt a table spoon of butter, and add one soupspoon of the batter.

4,Flip the crepe once bubbles appear on top of the crepe. When turned, distribute about 2 tablespoons of jam on the crepe. Repeat this process until you are out of batter.
